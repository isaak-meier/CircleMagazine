Three weeks off the grid taught me what I actually want from life
🎵
Dave Slater
5 min read  ·  Posted this morning
14
3

Somewhere around day five, I stopped reaching for my phone out of habit. Not because I was disciplined — there was no signal. But by the time I had bars again, I realized I hadn't missed it.

We drove out Friday night, nine of us crammed into two trucks, with enough food for a week and exactly zero plan. The campsite was three hours past the last gas station and had no name on the map — just a pin someone had dropped two summers ago and never deleted.

"The most productive thing I did all week was watch the same sunset twice from different angles."

By day three I'd stopped wearing a watch. By day five I couldn't have told you what month it was without thinking about it. That felt alarming for about thirty seconds, and then it felt like the best thing that had happened to me in years.

What I noticed, coming back: I wanted less. Not in a monastic way — I still want my coffee and my good speakers and my comfortable bed. But the twenty tabs of research I had open before I left, the four side projects I was "about to start," the mental queue of things I owed people — none of it felt urgent anymore. A lot of it turned out to just be noise.

Appears once the hero scrolls out of view. Keeps authorship anchored while reading — you always know whose piece this is. Reactions live here too so they're reachable mid-scroll without going back to the top.
